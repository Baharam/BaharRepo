Traffic Control

 

 

All personnel responsible for preparing and implementing worksite traffic management must be appropriately qualified. The minimum required training for various functions is outlined below:

Traffic control using stop/slow bat – all field personnel designated as a Traffic Controller must have undertaken a course in traffic control i.e. RIIOHS205A – “Control Traffic with a stop/slow bat” or equivalent, or an appropriate refresher course (within the last three years), from a Registered Training Organisation accredited under the Australian Qualifications Framework.

Traffic Guidance Scheme (TGS) implementation – all field personnel responsible for setting out, implementing and/or monitoring a TGS (as required by a traffic management plan) must have undertaken a course in TGS implementation e.g. RIIOHS302A – “Implement traffic management plan” or equivalent, or an appropriate refreshZer course (within the last three years), from a Registered Training Organisation accredited under the Australian Qualifications Framework.

Traffic Management Plan (TMP) preparation - all personnel responsible for preparing, approving, reviewing and/or making modifications to a x TMP (including the worksite hazard assessment) must have undertaken a course in TMP preparation e.g. RIICWD503A - “Prepare work zone traffic management plan”, or equivalent, or an appropriate refresher course (within the last three years), from a Registered Training Organisation accredited under the Australian Qualifications Framework OR have at least two years experience in preparing TMPs.
