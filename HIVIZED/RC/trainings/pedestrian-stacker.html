Forklift Training

 

 

Any person required to operate a forklift autonomously in Australia is required to hold a Licence to Perform High Risk Work.

High Risk Work Licence assessments must only be conducted by a Registered Training Organisation that is authorised by WorkSafe Victoria. As of October 1 2012 there are some mandated changes taking place regarding the training and assessment process' for high risk work.

Check the PDF "High Risk Work Process" below for information on the right process for you. At HiViz Ed we pride ourselves on delivering quality training and assessments in forklift operation. To ensure we give our students the best possible chance of passing their assessment we don't offer free re assessments, instead we offer quality training in small groups maximum 4 people.

We prefer to be proactive and give a small number of students a large amount of attention, rather than being pective and offering free re assessments to students who may not have received the attention they need to pass the assessment 1st time around. By no means does this ensure every student passes 1st attempt, just that we give every student the best possible chance to achieve thier forklift licence.

 

Onsite Forklift Licence Course Bayswater & Melbourne We can deliver forklift training and licence assessments to your site in Bayswater or anywhere in the Melbourne metro area. Courses will range from 1 -3 days depending on the operators experience and how many operators are attending the course.

Forklift Awareness for Pedestrians

 

 

This course is developed to provide operators of electric pallet movers with the underpinning knowledge required to safely operate this equipment. This course is suitable for experienced operators or first time users. Courses are delivered at the client’s site.

 

In Victoria between 1985 and 2006 there were 56 forklift related fatalities. Of these 30 were pedestrians, 16 were hit by loads and 14 were struck by a forklift (in addition to this 8 fell from forklift forks or load while being lifted).
This short course/information session informs pedestrians in the warehouse of the dangers that forklifts posses and to educate pedestrians on the operating characteristics of the forklift and how to avoid becoming another statistic.
Forklift braking distances, right of way and pedestrian accident statistics are just a few of the topics discussed in this information session. Duration is approximately 45 mins.

The Construction White Card

 

 

The Construction (OH&S Induction) Card (CIC) training establishes general, industry-wide OH&S training and is a requirement for all people who work on construction sites, both domestic and commercial.

The training was established by unions, employer associations, the Victorian Workcover Authority and the Office of the Chief Electrical Inspector and finalised by the Victorian Construction Industry Training Agreement in January 2001.

As of 1st July 2008, all White cards are issued through WorkSafe, however training will continue to be carried out by the HiViz Ed on their behalf. You will be required to bring photo identification to the course. At the end of the training, the HiViz Ed will issue you with a Statement of Attainment for CPCCOHS1001A Work Safely in the Construction Industry.

Traffic Control

 

 

All personnel responsible for preparing and implementing worksite traffic management must be appropriately qualified. The minimum required training for various functions is outlined below:

Traffic control using stop/slow bat – all field personnel designated as a Traffic Controller must have undertaken a course in traffic control i.e. RIIOHS205A – “Control Traffic with a stop/slow bat” or equivalent, or an appropriate refresher course (within the last three years), from a Registered Training Organisation accredited under the Australian Qualifications Framework.

Traffic Guidance Scheme (TGS) implementation – all field personnel responsible for setting out, implementing and/or monitoring a TGS (as required by a traffic management plan) must have undertaken a course in TGS implementation e.g. RIIOHS302A – “Implement traffic management plan” or equivalent, or an appropriate refresher course (within the last three years), from a Registered Training Organisation accredited under the Australian Qualifications Framework.

Traffic Management Plan (TMP) preparation - all personnel responsible for preparing, approving, reviewing and/or making modifications to a x TMP (including the worksite hazard assessment) must have undertaken a course in TMP preparation e.g. RIICWD503A - “Prepare work zone traffic management plan”, or equivalent, or an appropriate refresher course (within the last three years), from a Registered Training Organisation accredited under the Australian Qualifications Framework OR have at least two years experience in preparing TMPs.
